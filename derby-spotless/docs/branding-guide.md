# Brand Guide — Derby Spotless

## Positioning statement

Derby Spotless is the premium, reliable alternative to the informal "cash-in-hand cleaner" market and the impersonal national franchise. We sell **time, consistency and peace of mind** — not just a clean house. Every brand decision should reinforce: this is a professional, insured, accountable service you can trust with your home or business, priced fairly for the certainty it provides.

## Who we are not

- Not the cheapest option on the market, and we don't compete on price as the headline.
- Not a faceless franchise — customers get the same cleaner wherever possible, and a real local team behind the booking.
- Not informal or "side-hustle" in tone — no stock photos of mops and rubber gloves, no exclamation-point-heavy discount banners.

## Brand personality

| Trait | What it looks like in practice |
|---|---|
| Trustworthy | DBS checks, insurance, satisfaction guarantee stated plainly and often |
| Premium-but-approachable | Polished design and copy, but warm and human — never cold or corporate |
| Efficient | Instant Quote tool, fast response times, no unnecessary friction |
| Local | Named areas, named landmarks, real Derby context — never generic "your city" copy |

## Voice and tone

- **Outcome-first**: lead with what the customer gets back (time, a guest-ready home, peace of mind) before describing the mechanics of the clean.
- **Plain, confident, specific**: "Same cleaner for every visit, wherever possible" beats "We strive for excellence in service delivery."
- **No false urgency or hype**: avoid "LIMITED TIME," excessive capitalisation, or stacked exclamation marks. Confidence reads as premium; hype reads as cheap.
- **Second person, active voice**: "You get your weekends back," not "Customers can expect to reclaim leisure time."

## Visual identity

- **Colour system**: a confident brand colour (`brand-*` scale) paired with a near-black ink neutral scale (`ink-50`–`ink-950`) rather than pure black/white — this is what gives the site its premium, slightly editorial feel rather than a flat "corporate blue" template look.
- **Typography**: a display typeface (Manrope) for headlines paired with a clean, highly legible body typeface (Inter) — display type carries the brand's confidence, body type carries trust through readability.
- **Iconography**: a single consistent icon library (Lucide) throughout, never mixed with emoji or stock clip-art icons. Icons are functional wayfinding, not decoration.
- **Shape language**: soft, rounded corners (`rounded-xl`/`rounded-2xl`) and generous whitespace — signals calm and care, appropriate for a service entering people's homes.
- **No stock photography of generic models in rubber gloves.** If/when real photography is commissioned, prioritise: real Derby properties and landmarks, real (consented) team members in branded uniform, genuine before/after results. Authentic imagery is a premium differentiator versus competitors using obvious stock photos.

## Messaging pillars (use across ads, GBP, social, sales calls)

1. **Consistency** — the same vetted cleaner, every time, wherever possible.
2. **Accountability** — DBS-checked teams, full insurance, a real satisfaction guarantee with a re-clean promise, not just marketing language.
3. **Convenience** — instant online quotes, fast response times, simple digital booking and invoicing — no awkward cash-in-hand arrangements.
4. **Local expertise** — genuinely know Derby: the city centre, the suburbs, the landlord and student turnover cycles, the local property types.

## Naming and terminology conventions

- Always "Derby Spotless," never abbreviated to "DS" or "Spotless" alone in customer-facing copy.
- Service names are fixed and consistent everywhere (site, GBP, ads, invoices): Regular Domestic Cleaning, Deep Cleaning, End of Tenancy Cleaning, Landlord & Property Cleaning, Airbnb & Holiday Let Cleaning, Office & Commercial Cleaning, After Builders Cleaning, Carpet & Upholstery Cleaning, Elderly & Support Cleaning.
- Refer to staff as "cleaners" or "our team," never "maids" or "cleaning ladies" — both dated and at odds with the professional positioning.
- Prices are always presented as transparent and upfront ("fixed price, confirmed before booking"), never vague ("prices from" with no context).

## Do / Don't quick reference

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Get your weekends back." | "We do cleaning for you." |
| "Same cleaner for every visit, wherever possible." | "Cleaners may vary." |
| "Fixed price, agreed before we start." | "Prices may vary on the day." |
| Specific local detail (Pride Park, Allestree, Cathedral Quarter) | Generic "your area" copy |
| Calm confidence | Hype, urgency banners, excessive exclamation marks |
