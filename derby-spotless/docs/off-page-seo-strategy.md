# Off-Page SEO & Backlink Strategy — Derby Spotless

## Objective

Build domain authority and local relevance signals that get Derby Spotless ranking above national franchise cleaners and local competitors for commercial terms like "cleaning company Derby," "end of tenancy cleaning Derby," and area-specific variants across all 11 covered locations — without resorting to spammy, penalty-risk tactics.

## Guiding principle

Every link should be earnable in a world without SEO. If the only reason a link would exist is "it helps rankings," skip it. Prioritise links that also send real referral traffic or build genuine local trust.

## 1. Local citation foundation (Month 1)

Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across:

- Google Business Profile (primary — see `gbp-strategy.md`)
- Bing Places
- Apple Maps Connect
- Yell.com, Thomson Local, Cylex, Scoot
- Checkatrade, TrustATrader, Rated People (trade-specific directories with genuine review value)
- Derby-specific directories: Derby Telegraph business directory, Marketing Derby member listings, Derby & Derbyshire Chamber of Commerce (if joining as a member)

Use `Derby Spotless Ltd`, `Cardinal Square, 10 Nottingham Road, Derby, DE1 3QT`, `01332 696 911` identically everywhere. Inconsistent NAP is the single most common reason local rankings stall.

## 2. Niche & trade directories (Month 1–2)

- National Domestic Cleaning Association (or equivalent trade body) membership listing
- Insurance-linked directories (public liability insurers often list verified tradespeople)
- DBS-checker / safety verification badges that link back (e.g., a "DBS Verified" badge scheme) — dual purpose: trust signal on-site + backlink

## 3. Local link earning (Month 2–4)

- **Sponsor a local cause**: a Derby County or Derby City youth sports team, a Derby parkrun, or a local school fun-run. Sponsorship pages almost always link to the sponsor's site — and it's a genuine community presence that fits the premium, trustworthy brand position.
- **Supplier and partner cross-links**: reciprocal-but-natural links with non-competing local trades we'd genuinely refer customers to (letting agents for end-of-tenancy referrals, removal companies, handyman services). A landlord using our Landlord & Property Cleaning service is also likely searching for a letting agent — partner with 2–3 reputable Derby letting agents for a "trusted contractor" mention.
- **Local press**: pitch a data-led story to Derby Telegraph / Derbyshire Live — e.g., "Derby Spotless reveals the messiest rooms in Derby homes" or a survey of 500 local customers on cleaning habits. Local press loves a local data hook with quotes from a named local business owner.
- **Charity cleaning day**: an annual free deep-clean for an elderly resident or a local charity's premises, written up as a case study with photos, pitched to local news and shared by the charity (who will link from their own site/socials).

## 4. Content-led link earning (Month 3–6)

Tied to the blog (see `content-strategy.md`):

- Comprehensive, genuinely useful guides (e.g., the existing "How Much Does Cleaning Cost in Derby" pricing guide) are the most linkable asset type — landlords' forums, tenant advice sites, and local Facebook community pages will link to a clear, trustworthy local pricing breakdown.
- Outreach to Derby-focused lifestyle blogs and "moving to Derby" relocation guides offering the End of Tenancy Cleaning Checklist as a useful resource link.
- University of Derby and local student accommodation Facebook groups / forums — the end-of-tenancy checklist is highly relevant to the September/June student turnover periods.

## 5. Digital PR / guest contribution (ongoing)

- Guest articles on UK property and landlord publications (e.g., landlord-focused trade press) authored by the founder, with a bio link — positions Derby Spotless as an authority on rental property presentation, not just "another cleaner."
- Respond to journalist requests (via services like Response Source / Featured) on cleaning, home, and small-business topics — quick wins for high-authority links.

## 6. What to avoid

- Paid link networks, PBNs, or bulk directory submission services — these create unnatural link velocity and risk manual penalties.
- Guest post farms that accept any topic for a fee.
- Reciprocal link exchanges with unrelated/irrelevant sites purely for SEO value.

## Measurement

Track via Google Search Console (impressions/position for target local queries) and a backlink monitoring tool (Ahrefs or Semrush) for referring domain growth, monitored quarterly rather than obsessively week-to-week — local link velocity should look organic, i.e. gradual.
