# Google Business Profile Strategy — Derby Spotless

## Why this matters more than the website for local intent

For searches like "cleaner near me" or "end of tenancy cleaning Derby," the Google Map Pack (top 3 local results) gets the majority of clicks before anyone scrolls to organic results. GBP optimisation is the highest-leverage local SEO activity available.

## 1. Profile setup

- **Business name**: `Derby Spotless` — exactly, no keyword-stuffing ("Derby Spotless | Best Cleaners Derby" violates Google's guidelines and risks suspension).
- **Primary category**: House Cleaning Service.
- **Secondary categories**: Commercial Cleaning Service, Carpet Cleaning Service, Upholstery Cleaning Service — matching the actual service range so each can surface for category-specific searches.
- **Service area**: set explicitly to all 11 covered areas (Derby, Mickleover, Littleover, Chaddesden, Spondon, Allestree, Oakwood, Chellaston, Belper, Long Eaton, Ilkeston) rather than a generic radius, so Google understands precise coverage rather than guessing from a pin radius.
- **Address**: Cardinal Square, 10 Nottingham Road, Derby, DE1 3QT — verified via Google's postcard/phone verification.
- **Phone**: 01332 696 911 — must match the website exactly (including formatting) for NAP consistency.
- **Hours**: matched exactly to the site's opening hours (Mon–Fri 07:00–19:00, Sat 08:00–16:00, Sun closed).
- **Website link**: homepage; consider a UTM-tagged link (`?utm_source=gbp&utm_medium=organic`) to track GBP-driven traffic in analytics separately from organic search.
- **Booking link**: link directly to `/instant-quote` — Google surfaces a prominent "Book online" button when a booking URL is set, and the Instant Quote tool is the highest-converting entry point on the site.

## 2. Services list

Add every individual service as a distinct GBP "Service" entry (not just categories), mirroring the 9 service pages: Regular Domestic Cleaning, Deep Cleaning, End of Tenancy Cleaning, Landlord & Property Cleaning, Airbnb & Holiday Let Cleaning, Office & Commercial Cleaning, After Builders Cleaning, Carpet & Upholstery Cleaning, Elderly & Support Cleaning. Each with a short description (50–300 characters) using natural language matching the site's outcome-led copy, not generic descriptions.

## 3. Photos

Photos are a major trust and engagement driver in the Map Pack. Required shoot list:

- Branded uniform/team photos (cleaners arriving, in branded polo shirts) — humanises the "DBS checked, in-person interviewed" trust point.
- Before/after shots for deep cleaning, end of tenancy, and after-builders jobs — the single highest-engagement photo type for cleaning businesses on GBP.
- Branded van/vehicle photo if applicable.
- Office/Cardinal Square exterior photo for legitimacy.
- Logo and a square profile photo meeting Google's sizing guidelines.

Upload weekly, not all at once — a steady cadence of fresh photos signals an active, real business and keeps appearing in the profile's "recent" feed.

## 4. Reviews strategy

The site already centres around a 4.9 rating and 612+ reviews — GBP reviews must mirror and reinforce this.

- **Ask at the moment of highest satisfaction**: immediately after a completed clean, via an automated SMS/WhatsApp follow-up with a direct review link (use Google's short review link generator, not a generic profile link — fewer clicks to leave a review = higher completion rate).
- **Respond to every review within 24–48 hours** — thank positive reviewers by first name and service type (reinforces keywords naturally); respond to any negative review calmly, take ownership where warranted, and reference the 100% satisfaction guarantee/re-clean offer publicly so prospective customers see issues get resolved.
- **Never incentivise reviews** (gift cards, discounts for reviews) — this violates Google's policies and risks profile removal.
- Target a steady velocity of 15–25 new reviews per month across the customer base, rather than review-gating bursts.

## 5. Google Posts

Weekly posts (offers, blog cross-posts, seasonal reminders — e.g., "End of tenancy season is here: book your move-out clean 2 weeks ahead" timed to typical student/rental turnover in June and September) keep the profile active, which correlates with better local visibility.

## 6. Q&A section

Seed the Q&A section with the most common questions already answered in the site's FAQ data (e.g., "Do I need to be home during the clean?", "Are your cleaners insured?") — answered by the business account itself before competitors or low-quality auto-generated answers appear.

## 7. Messaging

Enable GBP messaging with quick auto-replies pointing to the Instant Quote tool or WhatsApp, since GBP message response time is a visible profile metric.

## 8. Ongoing maintenance cadence

| Frequency | Action |
|---|---|
| Daily | Monitor and respond to new reviews and messages |
| Weekly | Upload 1–2 new photos, publish 1 Google Post |
| Monthly | Review Insights (search queries, calls, direction requests) to spot which areas/services are under-indexed |
| Quarterly | Audit NAP consistency across GBP and all citation sources; refresh service descriptions |
